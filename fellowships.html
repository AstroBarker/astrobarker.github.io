Fellowship Resources

The NSF Graduate Research Fellowship

There is a wealth of advice out there on preparing NSF GRFP applications and I can’t really do it justice. The best resource for me was Mallory Ladd’s site. She covers it all. Also useful is Alex Lang’s site. There’s a huge list of essays here that may be helpful. I applied in October 2018 as a senior undergraduate and was awarded the fellowship in April. My research statement and personal statement are available as examples. You can find the reviewer’s feedback on my essays here. You can also find a template directory with LaTeX files that comply with proposal formatting guidelines courtesy of Carl Fields. Feel free to contact me if you have any other questions!

The FORD Foundation Predoctoral Fellowship

The FORD differs from the NSF in the emphasis that is placed on your potential as a future educator. Much of the previous advice for the NSF GRFP applies here as well, though some extra thought should be given in choosing your letter writers to ensure that they can properly address the criteria relevant for the FORD. It’s fairly straightforward to adapt an NSF proposal into a FORD proposal, just make sure that you address all of the points that they outline. Also check out Carl Field’s page for his advice and essays! I applied in December 2018 and received an an honorable mention in March.
