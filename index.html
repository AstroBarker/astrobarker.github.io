    I am a (soon-to-be) first year Astronomy and Astrophysics Ph.D student at Michigan State University working under Sean Couch as part of the SNAPhU research group. I am also currently collaborating with Eirik Endeve assisting with the development of the thornado supernova hydrodynamics code.

    I obtained my B.S. in physics in 2019 from the University of Tennessee, Knoxville working under Eirik Endeve and Anthony Mezzacappa developing supernova hydrodynamics algorithms and studying the supernova explosion mechanism.

    I am interested in stellar nucleosynthesis, the effects of nuclear physics on the outcomes of core collapse, and the explosion mechanism of core collapse supernovae.
