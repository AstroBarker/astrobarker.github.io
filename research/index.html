    Equation of State Dependence of Core-Collapse Supernova Observables

    The outcomes of CCSN simulations can depend sensitively on the equation of state (EOS). Therefore, a quantitative understanding of how different EOS tables affect the outcome of core collapse is crucial to our ability to make predictions. 1D CCSN simulations are ideal tools for understanding this sensitivity, as we can run thousands of 1D simulations to truly explore the parameter space in a meaningful way. Using a new model for creating physical explosions in 1D, we explore the sensitivity of CCSNe to variations in input nuclear physics. We have tested the sensitivities of the observable signals to the nuclear equation of state and explored correlations of the signals with fundamental nuclear physics quantities, such as the symmetry energy and effective nucleon mass.

    Supernova Hydrodynamics

    It is by now well understood that hydrodynamics plays a crucial role in CCSNe. Turbulence, convection, and hydrodynamic instabilities all play pivotal roles in driving the explosion. It is important, then, that the governing hydrodynamic equations are solved accurately and efficiently. The toolkit for high-order neutrino-radiation hydrodynamics (thornado) is being developed for CCSN simulations and related problems utilizing a spatial discretization based on the discontinuous Galerkin (DG) method. I am working to generalize the implementation in thornado to work with a tabulated nuclear matter equation of state (EOS). Moreover, I am implementing characteristic slope limiting, as this has been shown to be more effective than limiting on the conserved variables.
